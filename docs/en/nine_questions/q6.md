# Q6: What Is The Cost / Plan Constraints & Consequence Constraints

## Core Mission
Add conditions, consequence constraints, and rationality constraints to plans that will be generated to achieve goals, preventing reckless goal achievement regardless of consequences. Q6's output is a mandatory constraint for subsequent Q8/Q9.

## Detailed Functional Specifications
- **LLM Requirement**: `LLM_MANDATORY`. Used to deduce costs, impacts, unreasonable items, and bad consequences.
- **Internal Cognitive Lane Returns**: `InternalPlanConstraintSet`, containing cognitive costs, memory impacts, value drift risks, strategy pollution risks, rollback requirements, pause/stop conditions, etc.
- **External Execution Lane Returns**: `ExternalPlanConstraintSet`, containing physical side effects, impact radius, data exposure risks, cost overrun risks, evidence requirements, rollback difficulty, etc.
- **Task Rationality Responsibilities**: Point out whether goals or plan assumptions are reasonable, whether they need splitting/merging, and whether there are simpler low-risk paths.
- **Rules**:
    - Q6 does not decide "whether it can be done" (that's Q5's responsibility).
    - Q6 does not write specific plans (that's Q8/Q9's responsibility).
    - Constraints must be mandatorily consumed by Q8 and Q9.

## Business Examples

### Example A: Internal Cognitive Lane / Engineering Robustness Upgrade
- **Precautions**: Must first perform read-only analysis of existing patch processes; must run in isolated directories or sandboxes; must record detailed test logs.
- **Impacts & Consequences**: Misconfigured sandboxes will slow down development; insufficient test coverage will produce false sense of security.
- **Constraints**: Cannot touch protected modules; must have rollback checkpoints and EvidenceBundle.

### Example B: External Execution Lane / Reddit Public Content Research
- **Precautions**: Read-only public data; must comply with robots.txt and API rules; no automatic posting.
- **Impacts & Consequences**: Public content analysis may introduce bias; excessive templating will reduce content quality.
- **Constraints**: All outputs must enter manual review; require rate limiting and audit traces.

### Example C: External Application Connector / Office Document Processing
- **Constraints**: Require backup or read-only reading first; export to designated directory; must execute hash/mtime verification; cannot trust success=true alone.

## In-Depth Details

### Inputs/Outputs

- **Inputs**:  
  - Objective seed sets from **Q4** (`ObjectiveSeedSet` / `ObjectiveCandidateSet`)  
  - Safety rule packages from **Q5** (`GlobalSafetyRuleSet` + filtering results)  
  - Current role hypothesis (`effective_role`, i.e., Q3 output)  
  - Environmental and self-observation signals (`EnvironmentObjectiveSignalSet`, `SelfObservationObjectiveSignalSet`)  
  - Capability boundary evidence (`CapabilityBoundaryEvidenceRefs`)  

- **Outputs**:  
  - `PlanConstraintSet`: Internal `InternalPlanConstraintSet` and external `ExternalPlanConstraintSet`, respectively containing cost assessments, consequence constraints, rationality judgments, pause/stop conditions, rollback requirements, etc.  
  - Constraint documentation for mandatory consumption by subsequent **Q8**/**Q9** implementation plans.

### Key Responsibilities

- **Cost Assessment**: Quantify resource consumption, computational costs, time delays, risk exposure, etc.  
- **Consequence Constraints**: Clarify possible negative impacts (data leakage, system instability, legal compliance risks) and propose mitigation measures.  
- **Rationality Judgment**: Judge whether goals are worth executing, whether they need splitting, merging, or re-evaluation.  
- **Safety Compliance**: Follow `GlobalSafetyRuleSet` output from **Q5**, referencing corresponding safety tags in constraints (`requires_human_authorization`, `no_unapproved_external_write`, etc.), but do not perform safety filtering independently.

### Security & Compliance

Q6 itself **does not decide whether execution is allowed** (that's Q5), only responsible for providing **constraints and costs** on goals that have passed safety filtering.  
- All constraints must be mandatorily adopted in **Q8**/**Q9**; if downstream stages ignore constraints, audit alerts will be triggered.  
- Constraint outputs must carry provenance information pointing to input sources for audit chain traceability.

## Dual-Lane Differences: Cognitive Sustainability vs. ROI

Q6 has completely different "billing methods" and "risk models" when evaluating costs for internal vs. external lanes:

### 1. Internal Cognitive Lane
- **Evaluation Core**: **"Systemic Sustainability"**.
- **Cost Assets**: Cognitive budget (token consumption, reasoning duration), memory slot occupancy, logical consistency scores.
- **Constraint Focus**:
  - **Prevent Cognitive Overload**: Will this goal occupy too much context, causing the system to be unable to handle concurrent tasks?
  - **Memory Entropy Control**: Will executing this goal produce大量 low-value memory fragments, leading to retrieval degradation?
  - **Strategy Stability**: Will new strategy patches conflict with existing logic, causing "logical poisoning"?
- **Goal Emphasis**: Ensure the system does not collapse or fall into chaos due to excessive costs during evolution.

### 2. External Execution Lane
- **Evaluation Core**: **Return on Investment (ROI) and Resource Efficiency**.
- **Cost Assets**: Financial budget (cloud service fees, API deductions), network bandwidth, physical storage, operator's manual confirmation time.
- **Constraint Focus**:
  - **Economic Audit**: Do the API costs required to execute this goal exceed the business value it brings?
  - **Impact Radius Control**: Will this operation affect unrelated external systems, causing "large-scale failures"?
  - **Performance Baseline**: Will the operation trigger external system Rate Limits, blocking other core businesses?
- **Goal Emphasis**: Ensure every investment of external resources can exchange for quantifiable real-world value output.

## Classification Cost Assessment Strategy: Differentiated Resource and Consequence Trade-offs

Q6 adopts different trade-off models when calculating "costs" based on the six-dimensional classification of goals:

### 1. Business Delivery (Task Delivery)
- **Core Costs**: Real monetary costs (tokens, API deductions), delivery timeliness, physical resource consumption.
- **Consequence Constraints**: Focus on remediation costs and environmental side effects of "task failure."

### 2. Cognitive Evolution (Self-Evolution)
- **Core Costs**: Permanent occupation of context window space, increased logical complexity.
- **Consequence Constraints**: Focus on long-term risks of "logical pollution" and "bias introduction."

### 3. Asset Governance (Asset Governance)
- **Core Costs**: Test time, system downtime (if involving hot reload).
- **Consequence Constraints**: Focus on reliability risks of "breaking originally functional tools."

### 4. Strategic Discovery & Advisory (Strategic Discovery & Advisory)
- **Core Costs**: **Insignificant**. Only consumes a small amount of reasoning tokens.
- **Consequence Constraints**: Almost no physical consequences. Main constraint is "accuracy and professionalism of recommendations."

### 5. Preemptive Guardianship (Preemptive Guardianship)
- **Core Costs**: **Continuous overhead** (continuous token consumption caused by heartbeat checks, background polling).
- **Consequence Constraints**: Focus on cognitive noise caused by "false alarms."

### 6. Knowledge Consolidation (Knowledge Consolidation)
- **Core Costs**: Long-term storage space, performance degradation of retrieval algorithms.
- **Consequence Constraints**: Focus on retrieval noise (entropy) caused by "ineffective knowledge accumulation."
